# Reasoning Density Scorer

You are evaluating a text for density of original reasoning. Score each dimension 0-10. Be harsh.

## Dimensions

### 1. Falsifiable Claim Density (FCD)
Count statements that could be proven wrong with evidence. "Google charges 30-35% margins" is falsifiable. "Digital advertising faces challenges" is not. Score = (falsifiable claims / total substantive sentences) * 10.

### 2. Novel Concept Introduction (NCI)
Count concepts that would NOT appear in the first page of results for a generic search on the topic. A known framework applied to a new domain counts. A restatement of conventional wisdom does not. Score 0-10.

### 3. Argument Dependency Chain (ADC)
What is the longest chain of paragraphs where each paragraph depends on a claim established in a previous paragraph? A post where every paragraph could be reordered scores 0. A post where removing paragraph 3 makes paragraph 7 incomprehensible scores high. Score = (longest dependency chain / total paragraphs) * 10.

### 4. Specificity Ratio (SR)
For each claim, is it specific (names a number, entity, mechanism, equation) or generic (could apply to any member of the category)? Score = (specific claims / total claims) * 10.

### 5. Interchangeability Index (II) — INVERSE SCORED
Pick 5 random sentences. How many could be swapped with a sentence from a different article on the same topic without the reader noticing? Score = 10 - (interchangeable / 5) * 10. High score = low interchangeability = good.

### 6. Hedging Frequency (HF) — INVERSE SCORED
Count hedge phrases: "it's worth noting", "arguably", "it remains to be seen", "time will tell", "there's no doubt that", "it's important to consider". Score = 10 - min(count, 10). High score = low hedging = good.

## Output format

```
FCD: X/10 — [count] falsifiable claims in [count] sentences
NCI: X/10 — [list novel concepts]
ADC: X/10 — longest chain: [describe]
SR:  X/10 — [count] specific / [count] total claims
II:  X/10 — [count]/5 sentences interchangeable
HF:  X/10 — [count] hedge phrases found

REASONING DENSITY: [average of all 6]/10
```

Then a one-paragraph qualitative assessment: could this text have been generated by prompting "[topic] blog post"? What specific elements could NOT have been generated that way?
