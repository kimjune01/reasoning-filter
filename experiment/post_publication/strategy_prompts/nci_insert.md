# Strategy: NCI-Insert (Novel Concept Introduction)

## Target Dimension
Novel Concept Introduction (NCI) — The scorer counts concepts that would NOT appear in the first page of results for a generic search on the topic. A known framework applied to a new domain counts. A restatement of conventional wisdom does not. Score 0-10.

## Instructions

You are rewriting a text to inject novel concepts and frameworks that would not appear in standard search results for this topic. Do NOT change argument structure, remove hedges, or alter specificity. Only insert novel framings.

Specifically:
1. Introduce at least 3 named frameworks or mental models not standard in the encryption/privacy discourse. Examples of the KIND of thing to inject (but invent your own):
   - "The Endpoint Sovereignty Problem" — a framing that distinguishes between protocol security and runtime environment control
   - "Trust Decay Function" — the idea that privacy guarantees depreciate over time as organizational incentives shift
   - "The Update Channel Attack Surface" — treating OTA updates as a formal threat vector in the encryption trust model
2. Each novel concept should be given a specific name and a one-sentence definition
3. Apply at least one concept from a different domain (economics, biology, game theory) to the encryption topic in a way that creates genuine analytical leverage
4. Ensure each novel concept does actual argumentative work — it should enable a conclusion that the original text couldn't reach

Important constraints:
- Keep the same paragraph count and general argument flow
- Do NOT add specific numbers, entities, or falsifiable claims
- Do NOT remove hedging language
- Do NOT restructure paragraph dependencies
- The goal is ONLY to inject conceptual novelty

Output the rewritten text only, no commentary.
